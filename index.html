Their First Meeting

Toki was having a perfectly normal Tuesday. He had laced his skates with surgical precision, mapped his usual route around the Edinburgh rink, and was two peaceful laps in when — out of absolutely nowhere — a force of nature named Tife appeared and collided directly into him

She had never ice-skated before. She had, however, decided to figure it out that day with vibes and momentum. Unfortunately, momentum was the one thing she had too much of.

Toki would later describe the impact as "significant." Tife would later describe it as "fate." They are both correct.

He Taught Her to Skate. She Taught Him to Live.

Out of either immense kindness or a reluctance to leave a hazard unattended on the ice, Toki offered to teach Tife the basics. She held his arm the entire time — "for balance," she insisted. He didn't mind.

By the end of the session, she could glide approximately four metres without falling. He had laughed more than he had in months, A trade he considered more than fair.

She still can't skate that well. He still doesn't mind.

She Kept Disrupting His Routine. He Kept Letting Her.

Toki is, by nature, a man of order. Calendars and routines were his norm. Tife, by nature, is unpredictable, a little chaotic, yet somehow makes everything more interesting the moment she shows up.

She'd suggest spontaneous road trips on Sunday mornings. He'd already be up and had packed the car. She'd drag everyone onto the dance floor at parties; he'd eventually follow, reluctant but happy. She walks into rooms and rooms become better. He noticed that first. He kept it to himself for a while. Not for long though.

Everyone who knows Tife knows this about her; that's why she is nicknamed Sunshine.

And So — November 18th.

They will stand in front of you — their favourite people — and say the words. Toki will be calm. Tife will probably cry first and make him cry too, and then they'd laugh at each other about crying.

She disrupted his whole life on an ice rink in Scotland, and he has never once wished she hadn't. That is a love story if we've ever heard one.

You are invited to witness it. Try not to cry. (You will cry.)
